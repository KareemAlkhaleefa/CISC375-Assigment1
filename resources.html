Academic & Web Citations (ACM Format)

All content on this website was researched using the sources listed below. Citations follow the ACM (Association for Computing Machinery) reference format.

  1. Murray, H. J. R. 1913. A History of Chess. Oxford University Press, Oxford, UK. A foundational academic study of chess history, tracing its origins from India through Persia, the Islamic world, and medieval Europe.
  2. Brady, F. 2011. Endgame: Bobby Fischer's Remarkable Rise and Fall from America's Brightest Prodigy to the Edge of Madness. Crown Publishers, New York, NY. A definitive biography of Bobby Fischer, covering his rise to the World Championship and his turbulent later life.
  3. World Chess Federation (FIDE). 2024. Official FIDE Website — World Rankings and Championship Records. https://www.fide.com (Accessed: March 3, 2026). The official website of the Fédération Internationale des Échecs, providing current and historical world rankings, tournament results, and championship records.
  4. Chess.com. 2024. Chess History: The Complete Guide to the History of Chess. https://www.chess.com/article/view/history-of-chess (Accessed: March 3, 2026). A comprehensive overview of chess history, from Chaturanga to the modern era, written for a general audience. Chess.com is the largest online chess platform in the world with over 100 million members.
  5. Wikipedia contributors. 2024. Magnus Carlsen. Wikipedia, The Free Encyclopedia. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnus_Carlsen (Accessed: March 3, 2026). A comprehensive encyclopedic article on the career, playing style, and biographical background of the Norwegian chess grandmaster and former World Champion.
  6. Wikipedia contributors. 2024. Chaturanga. Wikipedia, The Free Encyclopedia. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chaturanga (Accessed: March 3, 2026). An encyclopedic article detailing the ancient Indian precursor to chess, including historical evidence, rules, and its relationship to other chess variants.
  7. Trigo, A. 2022. CSS Timelines: 10 Examples of Pure CSS Timeline Design. https://alvarotrigo.com/blog/html-css-timelines/ (Accessed: March 3, 2026). A comprehensive guide to building vertical and horizontal CSS timelines without JavaScript. The alternating left-right vertical timeline design used on this website's History Timeline page was inspired by examples presented in this article.
  8. Seirawan, Y. and Silman, J. 2003. Winning Chess Strategies (Revised Edition). Everyman Chess, London, UK. An instructional book covering fundamental chess strategy, including pawn structure, piece coordination, and planning — suitable for players of all levels.

Recommended External Resources

The following websites are recommended for anyone wishing to learn more about chess history, improve their play, or follow the modern professional chess scene:

  • Chess.com — The world's largest online chess platform, with over 100 million members. Offers lessons, puzzles, games, and extensive historical content.
  • Lichess.org — A free, open-source chess server with no advertisements and no paywalls. Features analysis tools, puzzles, and a large database of professional games.
  • ChessGames.com — A searchable database of over one million historical and contemporary chess games, annotated by the community. An invaluable research resource.
  • FIDE — World Chess Federation — Official website of the international governing body of chess. Provides official ratings, laws of chess, and tournament calendars.
  • Wikipedia: History of Chess — A thoroughly researched and extensively cited encyclopedic article covering the full 1,500-year history of chess, from Chaturanga to the computer age.
  • Encyclopaedia Britannica: Chess — A concise and authoritative encyclopedia entry covering the history, rules, and cultural significance of chess.

About This Website

The Royal Game is a multi-page static website created as a web development assignment. It was built using only HTML5 and CSS3 — no JavaScript, no CSS frameworks, and no third-party libraries were used. The site features semantic HTML5 markup, responsive design with CSS Flexbox and media queries, a pure CSS alternating vertical timeline, and typographic styling via Google Fonts.

Technical Standards

All pages on this website are written to conform to the following standards:

  • HTML5: Semantic markup using <header>, <nav>, <main>, <section>, <article>, and <footer> elements throughout.
  • W3C HTML Validation: All pages target compliance with the W3C Markup Validation Service at validator.w3.org.
  • W3C CSS Validation: Stylesheets target compliance with the W3C CSS Validation Service at jigsaw.w3.org/css-validator/ .
  • Accessibility: All images include descriptive alt attributes, heading hierarchy is logical and sequential, and ARIA landmark roles are used where appropriate.
  • Responsive Design: The site is designed to display correctly on mobile devices (max-width: 768px) and desktop screens (min-width: 769px), with no horizontal scrolling on any viewport size.
